About Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door

Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door is a highly efficacious Mahayana Buddhist practice transmitted by Guan Yin Bodhisattva to deliver sentient beings in this modern age, suited to the karmic conditions and spiritual capacity of contemporary practitioners.

The Three Golden Practices

Guan Yin Citta emphasizes the Three Golden Practices: sutra recitation, making vows, and life liberation (fangsheng). These methods enable practitioners to awaken and help others, freeing themselves from suffering and illness, resolving conflicts and karmic obstacles, and cultivating wisdom and inner peace.

Owing to its remarkable efficacy and swift results, Guan Yin Citta has touched the hearts of millions worldwide and continues to grow rapidly.

About Master Jun Hong Lu

Founder of Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door & Renowned Overseas Chinese Leader

Master Jun Hong Lu, the revered founder of Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door and a patriotic leader of the overseas Chinese community, peacefully entered Nirvana on November 10, 2021, at 4:00 a.m. in Sydney, Australia, at the age of 62, having fulfilled his vows in this world.

Master Lu began his Dharma propagation in 1997 and formally founded Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door in 2006. Over decades of tireless dedication, he compassionately guided countless sentient beings across the globe from confusion and suffering toward the path of enlightenment.

Master Lu's achievements in propagating Buddhism, preserving Chinese culture, and promoting interfaith harmony gained international recognition. He was repeatedly invited to deliver speeches by UNESCO and was highly respected in global religious and cultural circles.

Our Practice Center

Guan Yin Citta (NZ) Charitable Trust is the only officially designated Buddhist charitable organization of Guan Yin Citta Dharma Door in New Zealand, registered in Auckland. The trust is committed to promoting Buddhist culture, advancing community welfare, advocating vegetarianism and environmental awareness, and serving both local communities and those with karmic affinity.

Through a wide range of public-interest initiatives, the Trust actively promotes Buddhist values. These include the annual Vegetarian Carnival, as well as regular programs such as community Buddhist study sessions, health and wellness talks, and Chinese language classes for children.

Our Mission

Our trust follows the compassionate spirit of Guan Yin Bodhisattva, with a mission to promote true Buddhist teachings, encourage vegetarianism and environmental consciousness, and support the well-being of individuals and communities. By combining cultural education and community outreach, we aim to inspire kindness and build a society rooted in compassion, clarity, and wisdom.

We aspire to be a bridge between the wisdom of Buddhism and the broader public, practicing the spirit of the Bodhisattva path, forging wholesome connections, and helping more people attain happiness, clarity, and peace.
